    FFMpeg commands

    My most commonly used FFMpeg commands to manipulate audio / video files.

    Audio

    conversions

    • .mp3 to .og 

      ffmpeg -i input.mp3 output.ogg

    • .wav to .aac 

      ffmpeg -i input.wav -codec:a libfdk_aac -b:a 128k output.aac

    • extract audio from .mp4 to .mp3 

      ffmpeg -i input_video.mp4 -vn -codec:a libmp3lame -b:a 320k output.mp3

    filters

    Volume filter

    • Increase volume by 10 Decibals. 

      ffmpeg -i input.mp3 -af "volume=10dB" output_louder.mp3

    • Double volume of the audio. 

      ffmpeg -i input.mp3 -af "volume=2.0" output.mp3

    • Reduce volume by 50%. 

      ffmpeg -i input.mp3 -af "volume=0.5" output.mp3

    fading in and out

    • apply fade in over first 5 seconds 

      ffmpeg -i input.mp3 -filter:a "afade=t=in:ss=0:d=5" output_fade_in.mp3

    • Apply fade out over last half second of 10 second audio. 

      ffmpeg -i input.mp3 -af "afade=t=out:st=9.5:d=0.5" output.mp3

    tips and comments

    • -af is an abbriviation for -filter:a.
    • you can chain filters together in sequence using commas.

    cutting

    • Cut out the section between 0:30 and 1:45 from input.mp3 

      ffmpeg -ss 00:00:30 -to 00:01:45 -i input.mp3 -codec copy cut_input.mp3

    • Cut out a 30 second snippate from input.mp3 starting at 1 minute 

      ffmpeg -ss 00:01:00 -t 00:00:30 -i epic_tune.mp3 -codec copy snippet_epic_tune.mp3

    • Cut out a 5 second chunk starting at 5 seconds in input.mp3, and convert it into output.wav. 

      ffmpeg -ss 00:00:30 -t 00:00:05 -i input.mp3 output.wav

    tips

    • Remove -codec copy if you need to apply filters on the output, or convert to another format.
